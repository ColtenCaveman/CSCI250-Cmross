Bubble, Selection, and Insertion Sort are all pretty slow as your dataset gets big because there O of n^2. Bubble sort especially,
i feel goes without saying is basically just swapping till something works, so big datasets wont work well for it. Merge sort and 
quick sort are better as they are O of n log n, I do feel like merge sort is more consistent, as in my testing i would ovassionally
get a bigger variance in the time with quick sort, but i cant fully say why. The built in sort was super fast in all my testing and
nothing even came close, which after some research makes sense, it can vary from O of n in a best case to O of n log n which is
still pretty fast. All in all my timings made sense when compared to expected, Bubble, Selection and Insertion in the thousands of
milliseconds, bubble being the worst. Then merge and quick performing solid in the 10's of Ms, and Built in just crushing it at i
think the worst i saw was 1.5 but the lowest was like 0.4 which is insanely fast.
